Why

NALANDA FOUNDATION

As the digital economy accelerates ahead, not everyone is fortunate enough to be part of it. These include students from economically disadvantaged families and the poorer sections of society whose future looks dim as much as the new digital age shines in all its glory. Lack of exposure, no access to quality English-medium education and poor infrastructure severely impact their ability to keep up with their more privileged peers. This is the gap that Nalanda Education Foundation hopes to plug.

How + What

WHAT AND HOW WE

The 2019 Annual Status of Education Report throws up some stark realities. Barely a third of 60,000 students in rural India surveyed had access to online learning, with just 11% getting the opportunity to attend live virtual classes. The substandard level of education in rural schools is deeply apparent in the report which stated that more than half the students in Class 5 were unable to read a standard textbook from Class 2, or solve basic mathematical problems.
